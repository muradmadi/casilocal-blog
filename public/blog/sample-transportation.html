Getting Around Madrid: Transportation Guide for Students

By CasiLocal Team February 20, 2024

Madrid has an excellent public transportation system that makes getting around the city easy and affordable. Here's everything you need to know as a student.

Metro System

Abono Joven (Youth Pass)

The best deal for students under 26. For just €20/month, you get unlimited travel on:

  • Metro
  • Buses
  • Light rail (Cercanías) within Madrid

How to Get It: Bring your ID and proof of enrollment to any metro station
Savings: Can save €50+ per month compared to regular tickets

Single Tickets

If you don't travel much, single tickets cost €1.50-2.00 depending on zones. A 10-trip ticket (€12.20) offers better value.

Bus System

Madrid's bus network is extensive and complements the metro perfectly. The same Abono Joven works on buses.

Advantages:

  • Goes places metro doesn't reach
  • Great for seeing the city above ground
  • Night buses (Búhos) run all night

BiciMAD (Bike Sharing)

Madrid's public bike-sharing system is perfect for short trips and exploring.

Cost: €25/year for students
How It Works:

  • First 30 minutes free
  • €2 for each additional 30 minutes
  • Download the app to find and unlock bikes

Best For: Short trips, exercise, exploring neighborhoods

Walking

Madrid's city center is very walkable. Many students find they can walk to most places within 30 minutes.

Benefits:

  • Free
  • Great exercise
  • Discover hidden gems
  • Better for the environment

Taxis and Ride-Sharing

Regular Taxis

Taxis are metered and relatively affordable for short trips, but can add up quickly.

Base Fare: €2.50
Per Kilometer: €1.05

Uber/Cabify

Ride-sharing apps are available in Madrid and can be convenient, but public transport is usually cheaper.

Tips for Efficient Travel

  1. Get the Abono Joven – It's the best value if you use public transport regularly.

  2. Use Google Maps or Citymapper – These apps show the best routes combining metro, bus, and walking.

  3. Avoid rush hour – If possible, travel outside 7-9 AM and 6-8 PM to avoid crowds.

  4. Walk for short distances – If it's under 20 minutes, walking is often faster than waiting for transport.

  5. Combine transport modes – Use metro for long distances, bus for shorter trips, and walk for the last mile.
